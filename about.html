About Me

It's hard to write about yourself. I don't know if you've ever tried it, but everything sounds corny. Anyways, let's start with the basics.

  1. home
  2. articles
  3. about-me

I'm Nathan—a computer scientist—and I care a lot about helping people. Currently, I work on embedded operating systems. It's not where I thought I would be a decade ago, but it's a very a good place to be.

I spend a lot of time learning about new topics. It helps with conversations.

Things I love talking about: fonts, kernels, Kierkegaard, art, hip hop, architecture, design, anti-consumption, cooking, my family, Yeshua. (Articles on each to come.)

Things I have to fake interest in: Star Wars and JavaScript.

Sorry if that's hard to hear (either the Star Wars or the kernel part).

Hardware and software are both of interest to me. High level design feels familiar for me and low level fascinates me—it's a healthy mix of happy-comfort and scary-learning to switch between the two.

I've been building websites from scratch with HTML, CSS, and Javascript since middleschool. I've dabbled in frameworks, but prefer the simplicity of building from scratch. Plus, building with Angular won't get you in the 1 MB Club.

In high school, I thought I would become a web-developer or a graphic designer. My senior year of college, I designed, assembled, and programmed a single-board micro-computer from scratch. Funny how things change.

I'm very passionate about helping people learn and a core belief of mine is that learning should be accessible to everyone. My dream is to end up working in a field where I can mix the open source mantra with education.
